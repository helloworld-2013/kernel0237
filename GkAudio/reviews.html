Esotec System 242

  • Admin
  • May, 2014
  • Dynaudio

To accurately measure the qualities of the Dynaudio System 242, we set out to ensure each component would offer up its best performance traits. As the Dynaudio components were being broken in on our bench, we took the opportunity to audit their supplied specifications.
Read More...

Esotec System 242

Md 102 Soft Dome tweeters
Their frequency response was smooth and linear (with the microphone placed 30 degrees off-axis) from 2kHz to 16kHz, at which time they rolled off gently towards 20kHz. Their on-axis response was similar; however, the overall frequency response was extended to 23kHz before rolling off.
Mw 162 Mid-Bass driver
Dynaudio depicts the MW 162’s linear excursion limit as being 6mms peak-to-peak, with 17mms being the actual mechanical limit. During our investigation, we discovered that the MW 162s, in terms of distortion, remained sonically acceptable well beyond their 6mm X-max specification (8.3mms Peak-to-Peak). The speed of the drivers was also beyond expectations. While electrical delays on their own are not necessarily a bad thing, they are however a detriment to the overall stereo experience when some components within the system are operating at different times. The sound stage can collapse, bass performance can diminish and echoes can spear the intelligibility of vocals. The Dynaudio MW 162s make a positive contribution to time (phase) management, measuring virtual zero laten- cies above 82Hz. The frequency response was measured at 30 degrees off-axis (to mimic typical installations), revealing an extremely smooth and linear response from 60Hz-4kHz, producing a ruler-flat response from 1,822Hz-3,465Hz. At 0 degress deviation, the response picked-up slightly, with the frequency extension reaching 4,423Hz @ -3dB.
Esotec X 252 crossover
We observed a phase adjustment made to the Dynaudio MW 162s and a well-managed frequency crossover between the MW 162s and the MD 102s, occurring at 2,834Hz. The MD 102s appear to have a 12dB / octave HP and the MW 162s appear to have a 6dB / octave LP. Ultimately, with the X 252 at the heart of the Dynaudio System 242, the frequency response and overall timing qualities seemed to be very well engineered and frankly, exceptional!
